Dr. Toufique Ahmed
Associate Professor | Materials Informatics | AI in Textiles Researcher
I am an Associate Professor and PhD researcher specializing in the intersection of Textile Engineering and Machine Learning. My work focuses on leveraging artificial intelligence—specifically Artificial Neural Networks (ANN), XGBoost, and Deep Learning—to optimize manufacturing processes, enhance sustainability, and develop smart functional materials.

**🚀 Research Focus**

Materials Informatics: Accelerating the discovery of advanced materials using data-driven frameworks.

Predictive Modeling: Applying ML to reduce manufacturing defects (e.g., starting marks in looms) and optimize yarn production.

Sustainability: Developing AI systems to predict and minimize cumulative waste in the weaving and knitting industries.

Smart Textiles: Integrating nanotechnology (green synthesis of AgNPs) with computational analysis for antimicrobial and functional applications.

**🛠️ Technical Toolkit**

Programming: Python (TensorFlow, Scikit-learn, OpenCV, Pandas, NumPy), R-Programming, SQL.

Data Science: Statistical analysis with Statsmodels, SciPy, and Tidyverse.

Deployment: Building ML-driven web applications using Flask and HTML/CSS.

Scientific Writing: Expert proficiency in LaTeX for journal and book chapter preparation.
